## The Fundamental Challenge

### What We've Learned

**The problem is NOT discretization** - it's **taking minimum over noisy estimates**.

Both type-level AND observation-level fail for the same reason:
1. Estimate treatment effects (noisy)
2. Compute concordances (products amplify noise)
3. Take minimum via optimization
4. Selection picks most negative estimation errors
5. Result: **Systematic downward bias**

### Why Current Fixes Are Insufficient

**Conservative correction helps but is incomplete:**
- Adds penalty: phi_corrected = phi_naive + k*SE
- Reduces bias from -0.064 to -0.027 (58% improvement)
- But residual bias remains
- Coverage improves from 39% to 78%, still below 95%

**The issue:** Estimation noise creates *local* biases in concordances. The DRO optimization finds regions with downward-biased estimates. A global correction (adding k*SE to the final answer) doesn't fully fix local biases.

### Possible Solutions Forward

1. **Larger penalty** (k>3): May overcorrect in some cases
2. **Bayesian DRO**: Integrate over posterior uncertainty
3. **Robust estimation**: M-estimators for treatment effects
4. **Accept conservative estimates**: Document that method is intentionally conservative
5. **Alternative estimand**: Estimate something other than minimax?
