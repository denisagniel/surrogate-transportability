## Key Technical Contributions

### 1. Neyman Orthogonality Under Reweighting (Lemma 2.1)
**Original contribution:** Proved that standard AIPW orthogonality survives multiplication by importance weights $w(X; \mathcal{Q})$ because weights don't depend on nuisance parameters $\eta$.

**Why it matters:** Eliminates first-order nuisance bias, leaving only second-order remainder $O(\|\hat{\eta} - \eta_0\|^2)$.

### 2. Deterministic Uniformity via Bounded Weights (Lemma 2.2)
**Key insight:** Bounded second derivatives + bounded weights give remainder bound independent of MCMC sample index $m$.

**Consequence:** No Donsker theory or maximal inequalities needed. Works for any $M$ (even $M \to \infty$).

### 3. Sharp Rate Analysis
$o_P(n^{-1/4})$ nuisance convergence rates are information-theoretically optimal under standard smoothness conditions.
