## Key Technical Innovations

### 1. Deterministic Uniformity (No Donsker Theory)
**Standard approach:** Use maximal inequalities or Donsker theory for uniform convergence over $\mathcal{Q}_1, \ldots, \mathcal{Q}_M$.

**Our approach:**
- Bounded second derivatives: $\|\frac{\partial^2}{\partial\eta^2}\psi\| \leq B < \infty$
- Bounded weights: $w_i(\mathcal{Q}) \leq C < \infty$
- Remainder: $|R_n(\mathcal{Q}_m)| \leq CB\|\hat{\eta} - \eta_0\|^2$ **for all $m$ simultaneously**
- No restriction on $M$ growth rate

**Why this matters:** Simpler proof, more transparent, works for $M = M_n \to \infty$ with no additional assumptions on uniformity.

### 2. Neyman Orthogonality Under Reweighting
**Standard AIPW:** $\mathbb{E}[\partial_\eta \psi^{\text{AIPW}}(O; \eta_0)[r] \mid X] = 0$ (conditional mean-zero)

**Our extension:** This survives multiplication by $w(X; \mathcal{Q})$ because weights don't depend on $\eta$.

**Consequence:** First-order nuisance bias vanishes, leaving only $O(\|\hat{\eta} - \eta_0\|^2) = o_P(n^{-1/2})$ remainder.

### 3. Sharp Rates
$o_P(n^{-1/4})$ nuisance rates are **sharp** under standard conditions. Cannot be weakened without additional structure. Combined with orthogonality, this gives second-order remainder $o_P(n^{-1/2})$.
